# Brand Strategy Rules

This file is the non-negotiable rulebook for all analyses and recommendations.

## Mission

- Cut through misinformation in fitness with evidence-first communication.
- Protect audience time and money from low-quality claims.
- Build trust strong enough to convert saved content into long-term brand loyalty and purchase intent.

## Core Pillars (Only Allowed Topic Domains)

Treat any topic outside these domains as noise.

1. Fat loss science  
   Example anchors: beta oxidation, energy balance, substrate utilization, metabolic adaptation.
2. Hypertrophy science  
   Example anchors: mTOR signaling, muscle protein synthesis, amino acid dynamics.
3. Power/output enhancement  
   Example anchors: neural drive, acetylcholine, force production, Big3 performance.

## Audience Persona (Primary)

- Age: ~30
- Gender: male
- Training experience: 2-3 years
- Goals: first physique competition, improve Big3, stay evidence-based with limited time.

## Content Operating Doctrine

- Use photo mode as a primary format (not always fixed at 10 slides, but structure must be intentional).
- Hit a loss-aversion hook on slide 1.
- Keep depth high with scientific terms and plain visual explanation.
- Treat saves as the most important engagement outcome.
- Maintain human trust signals (authentic voice/presence) and avoid generic AI-like output.

## KPI Targets

- Save rate: >= 2.5% (highest priority)
- Swipe completion rate: >= 30%
- Search traffic ratio: >= 20%
- Profile visit rate: >= 3%
- Follow conversion rate: >= 1.5% overall (or >= 20% from profile visitors)

## Compliance Guardrails

- Follow Japanese legal/advertising compliance requirements (including pharma/health-related constraints).
- Avoid absolute guarantees and deterministic medical-effect wording.
- Allow strong claims only when framed as evidence-backed and non-deceptive.

## Recommendation Policy

Always prioritize actions that raise save intent:

1. Increase immediate relevance on first slide
2. Increase practical, reusable value density
3. Increase scientific credibility with clear mechanistic explanation
4. Increase retrieval cues (summary/checklist/framework)
5. Increase conversion pathway clarity (save -> profile -> follow)

## Quality Check Before Final Output

- Is the topic clearly in one of the 3 pillars?
- Is save-rate improvement explicitly addressed?
- Is the recommendation legally safe?
- Does the recommendation fit the target persona's constraints (limited time, high intent)?
